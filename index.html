Holman 100:
A Century of Surgery

Join us to celebrate one hundred years of Stanford Surgery — honoring the legacy of Emile Holman and the generations of surgeons, scholars, and healers who followed. We look forward to welcoming you on Friday and Saturday.

The Weekend

Join us to celebrate 100 years of Stanford Surgery: August 28–29, 2026.

The journey begins on Friday evening, with immersive, division-specific programs designed to bridge our past with our future.

On Saturday, we gather for a rare look into the journey of Stanford Surgery over the last century, from Emile Holman's time as chair in 1926 to today. Beyond the public milestones, we will explore the untold stories and transformational figures whose quiet brilliance shaped our departments. The celebration will culminate with a gala that evening, to reconnect old friends and create new connections throughout Stanford.
