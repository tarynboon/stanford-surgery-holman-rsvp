The Centennial

Where precision
meets purpose.

Stanford Surgery traces its roots to 1908, when Stanford took over Cooper Medical College in San Francisco, and to the formal founding of its surgical division in 1926.


Through a century of firsts — the first West Coast kidney transplant, the first U.S. adult heart transplant, the CyberKnife, and beyond — Stanford Surgery has led medicine from the operating room. The Holman 100 Centennial gathers our community of faculty, alumni, and friends to honor that legacy and launch the next century with the Holman 1-10-100 Fundraising Pledge.
